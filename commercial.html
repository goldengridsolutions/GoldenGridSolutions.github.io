The Problem

HVAC dominates consumption.
Most of it is waste.

In commercial buildings, centralized chilled water HVAC plants run continuously to serve offices, common areas, and tenant spaces. The majority of that energy is lost to thermal inefficiency before it reaches the occupied space.

🌡️
Chiller plants run far below peak efficiency
Most commercial chiller systems use conventional water or water-glycol fluid that was specified decades ago. Heat transfer performance degrades over time, forcing compressors to work harder — consuming more energy to deliver the same cooling output year after year.
🏢
No downtime window for equipment upgrades
Replacing or overhauling a chiller plant means shutting down HVAC across occupied tenant spaces — commercially and contractually untenable for most building owners. Any solution that requires system downtime or equipment modification simply isn't viable.
📊
ESG reporting requirements are accelerating
REIT investors, institutional landlords, and major corporate tenants increasingly require verified Scope 2 emissions data and ENERGY STAR performance. Auditable, third-party-verified energy reduction is moving from a differentiator to a lease requirement in Class A markets.
