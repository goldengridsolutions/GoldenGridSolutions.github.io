The Problem

The highest refrigeration loads
in any sector.

Cold storage and logistics facilities run heavy freezer rooms and chilled bays around the clock — 365 days a year. Compressors account for 80–90% of refrigeration energy, and most run harder than they need to on every single cycle.

🧊
Freezer rooms consume enormous power continuously
Large distribution centers run freezer rooms drawing 68 kW or more per unit — operating 24 hours a day, every day of the year. Even a modest improvement in compressor efficiency delivers hundreds of thousands of kWh in annual savings across a single facility.
📈
Seasonal variation makes energy unpredictable
Refrigeration loads spike by 25% or more in warmer months as ambient temperatures rise. This seasonal swing makes energy budgeting difficult and drives compressors to their limits during peak summer periods — the highest-cost time of year.
⚡
Dock equipment and motors add to the electrical load
Beyond refrigeration, cold storage sites run dock levelers, conveyor systems, forklifts, lighting, and loading equipment — all generating electrical noise that wastes energy upstream. Optimizing refrigeration alone leaves significant savings unrealized.

Global Food Distribution Center · Large Refrigerated Warehouse
"With refrigeration representing the majority of total electrical usage, even small efficiency gains make a material financial impact — and the results far exceeded expectations."

A major food distribution center operating five chilled cold rooms and three high-load freezer systems deployed enPact Refrigeration & Cooling across all eight units. Freezers were operating at 68.5 kW each — making refrigeration by far the dominant energy load on site. The technology uses AI-driven optimization to reduce compressor runtime using superheat-based control, while maintaining exact setpoint temperatures throughout.

The pilot ran across 34 days in winter, capturing 26.5% refrigeration energy reduction and more than 219 kWh saved per day across the freezer rooms alone. Because refrigeration loads increase 25% in warmer months, full-year savings were adjusted upward: 112,432 kWh annually, with a projected 11-month payback.

Applied across the operator's 340-site portfolio, the verified per-site result projects to 34.4 million kWh saved annually — one of the highest-impact opportunities documented in the refrigeration category.
